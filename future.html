After completing my PhD, I intend to establish myself in the private sector and develop an architecture studio that specializes in heritage conservation and adaptive reuse or “Bauen im Bestand”, while at the same time continuing my commitment to the communities of southern Mexico as an independent archaeologist with my own line of participatory research.

Participatory approaches may have profound implications for the field of heritage preservation in the near future. For almost a century, heritage protection in Mexico has been under the authority of the national government, but this centralized, top-down orientation of cultural heritage management has largely excluded the rights of Indigenous peoples regarding their own heritage. With the policies of recent left-leaning governments and the enactment of corresponding legislation, the position of Indigenous communities has been strengthened in recent years. Looking ahead, there may be increasing potential to incorporate their needs, wishes, and visions for the future into the management of their cultural heritage.
